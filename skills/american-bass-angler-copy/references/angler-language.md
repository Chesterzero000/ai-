# U.S. Bass Angler Language Reference

## Audience Assumptions

Default to U.S. freshwater bass fishing unless the user specifies another market. Common contexts include largemouth bass, smallmouth bass, ponds, lakes, reservoirs, rivers, bank fishing, kayak fishing, and boat fishing.

## Useful Angler Terms

Use terms naturally, not as keyword stuffing.

- Water and fish location: cover, structure, grass, weeds, laydowns, docks, brush piles, rock, riprap, points, flats, ledges, creek channels, drop-offs, humps, pockets, backs of creeks.
- Fishing behavior: cover water, slow down, pick apart cover, make long casts, stay on fish, find the pattern, dial in the bite, trigger reaction bites, finesse them, power fish.
- Sonar and mapping: mark fish, scan water, read bottom, find depth changes, map structure, see contours, locate high-percentage spots, save waypoints, find offshore structure.
- Tackle: jig, crankbait, jerkbait, spinnerbait, chatterbait, swimbait, topwater, frog, worm, soft plastic, Texas rig, Carolina rig, drop shot, Ned rig, shaky head.
- Conditions: stained water, clear water, muddy water, shallow, deep, pressured fish, post-front, low light, warm water, cold water.

## Strong Phrase Patterns

- Spend less time guessing and more time fishing.
- Find high-percentage spots before you make your first cast.
- See depth changes, bottom contour, and fish-holding structure.
- Built for bank, kayak, and small-boat anglers.
- Dial in where bass are holding.
- Cover water faster without fishing blind.
- Make smarter casts from the bank or boat.
- Turn unknown water into a mapped fishing spot.
- Save productive areas and come back to them with confidence.

## Weak or Unnatural Phrases to Avoid

- "Lure fishing users"
- "Fishing enthusiasts can enjoy intelligent fishing"
- "Explore the mystery underwater"
- "Make fishing more happy"
- "Professional fishing artifact"
- "Let you easily master fish information"
- "Open your smart fishing journey"
- "Catch more fish guaranteed"
- "Never miss a fish"
- "The best product for all anglers"

## Claim Guardrails

Avoid guarantee language unless the source explicitly supports it.

- Do not say "catch more fish guaranteed."
- Do not claim tournament approval, pro endorsement, military-grade durability, waterproof rating, battery life, app compatibility, range, depth, or accuracy unless provided.
- Prefer softer, believable claims: "helps you find," "makes it easier to see," "designed to help," "built for," "gives you a clearer read."

## Tone Variants

### Website

Clear, confident, lightly technical. Use benefits first.

### Amazon Listing

Keyword-aware, scannable, specific. Use bullets and concrete use cases.

### Paid Ads

Short, punchy, benefit-led. One idea per line.

### Email

Conversational and practical. Tie the product to a fishing scenario.

### Social

Casual, vivid, and compact. Avoid sounding like a catalog.
