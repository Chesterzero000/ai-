# American Bass Angler Copy

## Purpose

Use this skill to turn product or marketing copy into English that feels native to U.S. bass fishing customers, especially largemouth and smallmouth bass anglers. Prioritize credibility, clarity, and buying intent over literal translation.

## Core Workflow

1. Identify the target surface: landing page, product page, Amazon listing, ad, email, social post, packaging, or app copy.
2. Identify the likely angler: bank fisherman, kayak angler, boat angler, weekend bass angler, tournament angler, beginner, or gift buyer.
3. Preserve the product truth: do not invent specs, tournament claims, waterproof ratings, compatibility, discounts, reviews, or endorsements.
4. Rewrite in natural American fishing English.
5. Tighten for conversion: lead with the fishing benefit, support with the product feature, then add the action.
6. If the source copy is awkward or vague, provide a cleaner version and briefly note what changed.

## Voice

Write like a knowledgeable U.S. bass angler who also understands ecommerce.

- Use clear, direct, confident language.
- Sound practical and field-tested, not poetic.
- Favor "you" and outcome-driven phrasing.
- Keep sentences short for landing pages and ads.
- Use fishing terms only where they fit the product and audience.
- Avoid hype that sounds fake: "revolutionary," "ultimate," "unleash," "game-changing," "dominate," unless the user asks for aggressive ad copy.
- Avoid generic outdoor copy that could fit hiking, camping, or boating. Make the copy feel specifically useful on the water.

## Localization Rules

- Prefer "bass anglers" over "lure users" for U.S. bass contexts.
- Use "fishing spots," "cover," "structure," "drop-offs," "points," "flats," "ledges," "brush piles," and "grass lines" when describing where fish hold.
- Use "find fish," "mark fish," "dial in depth," "cover water," "make better casts," "fish with confidence," and "spend less time guessing" for sonar and mapping benefits.
- Use "bank fishing," "kayak fishing," "boat fishing," and "shore fishing" according to the product context.
- Use U.S. units when needed: feet, inches, pounds, ounces, miles, Fahrenheit.
- Use U.S. spelling: favorite, color, optimize, center.
- For ecommerce CTAs, use direct buttons: "Shop Now," "Buy Now," "Add to Cart," "Compare Models," "Learn More," "Find Your Setup."

## Output Formats

When rewriting a short phrase or section, provide:

```text
Rewritten:
[copy]

Why it works:
[1-3 concise notes]
```

When rewriting a full landing page section, provide:

```text
Section:
[section name]

Headline:
[headline]

Subheadline:
[subheadline]

Body:
[body copy]

CTA:
[button text]

Notes:
[brief rationale]
```

When translating Chinese source copy, do not produce a literal translation first unless the user asks. Go straight to natural U.S. bass angler copy.

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing copy, check:

- Does it sound like it was written for American bass anglers?
- Is the benefit clearer than the feature?
- Would a real angler understand the use case immediately?
- Is the claim supported by the provided source?
- Is there any Chinese-English phrasing, filler, or AI-sounding hype?
- Is the CTA appropriate for the user's page or channel?
